Who Australia beats — and who fights back

This diverging bar chart compares Australia’s all-time Test cricket wins and losses against each opponent, with wins extending to the right and losses to the left. The visualisation highlights Australia’s overall dominance in Test cricket, particularly against England, where Australia has recorded 156 wins despite also having the highest number of losses (113) due to the long history of the Ashes rivalry. Against Bangladesh and Zimbabwe, Australia has remained almost undefeated, reflecting a clear imbalance in competitiveness.

A global win rate

The map shades each Test nation by the share of matches Australia has won against them. The colour deepens against sides Australia has historically controlled: NewZealand and Sri Lanka sit among the darkest, with win rates above 54%. England, the oldest and most-played rival, holds Australia closest at around 43% — proof that the Ashes remains cricket's most even contest. India, too, has emerged as a genuine modern challenger, the only major side against whom Australia wins fewer than half its Tests.
