From triumph to collapse

Australian batting has known both glory and disaster: a towering 758 against the West Indies in 1955, down to an infamous collapse for just 36 against England in 1902. The dashed line marks 400 — a total Australia has passed only at its peak. Every one of its record lows came against England or South Africa. Data sourced from Wikipedia.

Two at the crease

Test cricket is built not on individuals alone, but on partnerships — two batsmen building an innings together. Australia's five greatest stands are a roll-call of the game's titans.

One name dominates: Sir Donald Bradman, shown in gold, appears in three of the top five. His 451-run stand with Bill Ponsford against England at The Oval in 1934 stood as a world record for two decades. The only modern entry — Ricky Ponting and Michael Clarke's 386 against India in 2012 — shows how rare such monumental partnerships have become.

That so many of these records date to the 1930s and 40s speaks to an era when Australia's batting, led by Bradman, simply overwhelmed opponents. Data sourced from Wikipedia.

Who Australia beats — and who fights back

This diverging bar chart compares Australia's all-time Test wins and losses against each opponent — wins extend right, losses left. It highlights Australia's overall dominance, particularly against England, where Australia has recorded 156 wins despite also having its highest loss count (113), a product of the long Ashes rivalry. Against Bangladesh and Zimbabwe, Australia has remained almost undefeated. Data sourced from ESPNcricinfo Statsguru.

A global win rate

The map shades each Test nation by the share of matches Australia has won against them. The colour deepens against sides Australia has historically controlled: New Zealand and Sri Lanka sit among the darkest, with win rates above 54%. England, the oldest and most-played rival, holds Australia closest at around 43% — proof that the Ashes remains cricket's most even contest. India, too, has emerged as a genuine modern challenger, the only major side against whom Australia wins fewer than half its Tests.
